Data up to Feb 29 2020


Key Takeaways
  • South Korea has seen escalation of Corona Virus since the 3rd week of Feb, and Sandalwood has various data sources - S. Korea Export, DFS, and local FMCG sales - to quantitatively monitor the current situation, as well as the recovery in the future.
  • Up to Feb 29, South Korea Export is not heavily impacted by Corona Virus in Jan and Feb, likely due to export nature - it is more focused on B2B raw/semi-finished goods so the hit is likely to be lagged. However, export to China is below seasonal, and overall export is expected to face headwind in Mar as 1) a glimpse of softness was seen in the last week of Feb, 2) weakening retail business will likely translate to export, 3) business exchange between S. Korea and other countries freeze, and 4) global supply chains have been disrupted by the outbreak in China in Feb, so S. Korea will likely have delayed impact in March.
  • Foreigners’ Duty-Free spending hit hard negatively, well-below seasonal during the 3 weeks following Chinese New Year, as 1) Chinese tourists inbound has been muted since late Jan due to virus outbreak in China, and 2) more and more countries are restricting citizens from traveling to S. Korea since late Feb. Amorepacific, LG H&H, and others with DFS as one of key channels face major headwind in 1Q20.
  • Locals’ spending on Grocery and Food products ramps up significantly due to panic buy since the 3rd week of Feb, the same week Corona Virus spread escalated.

Note: All index charts use prior year’s December daily average value as base 100. For example, if Dec 2019 daily average value is 30m and Feb 1-10 daily average value is 36m, in the index number for Feb 1-10 will be 36/30*100 = 120. Weekly value are adjusted to daily average first (because the weekly data is arranged as 1-10, 11-20, 21-month end), then indexed for like-for-like comparison. 

Export Trend - Export Data
  • Export from S. Korea to Global was largely seasonal during Jan and Feb this year, similar level as prior years, with a glimpse of softness seen at the end of Feb.
  • Export from S. Korea to Mainland China was below seasonal for the most part of Jan and Feb. Export to Hong Kong was slightly above seasonal, likely because more export to Mainland China were re-routed via Hong Kong compared to prior years. More than 80% of export from S. Korea to Hong Kong were re-exported to Mainland China in 2018.
  • Export from S. Korea to USA and Vietnam during Jan and Feb were seasonal and slightly above seasonal, respectively, with a hint of softness at the end of Feb.
  • Compared to retail demand, which saw instant negative impact from virus outbreak, export remain largely stable in Feb as it is more focused on B2B raw/semi-finished goods business. However, as the virus continues to spread in S. Korea in early Mar, retail demand is expected to worsen, and headwind in export is expected to follow.



Duty Free Sales Trend - DFS Data
  • Foreigners’ spending at Duty-Free stores in S. Korea remained sluggish in the 3 weeks (W+1, W+2, and W+3) following CNY week (W), significantly below seasonal. KDFA foreigner DFS sales is expected to decelerate to double-digit negative y/y in Feb.
  • Usually the weeks following CNY are peak travel season for Chinese tourists, however Chinese tourists inbound was muted this time due to virus outbreak in China. 
  • The prospect for DFS spending in Mar also looks gloomy as travel to and from S. Korea becomes stringent and restrictive since late Feb.
  • Amorepacific and LG H&H DFS revenue are expected to decelerated to double-digit negative y/y in 1Q20.


Local Supermarket Sales Trend - Supermarket Data
  • Local spending on Food and Grocery ramped up, above seasonal, since 3rd week of Feb (W+4 and W+5), as consumers hoard necessities such as food, masks, sanitizers, and cleaning products.
  • In food sector, instant noodles, rice, and canned foods were among the most hoarded products. Main beneficiaries were Nongshim, Samyang Foods, Ottogi, CJ Cheiljedang, and Dongwon F&B.
  • In grocery sector, masks, soap & hand wash, and house cleaning products were among the most hoarded products. Main beneficiaries were Kimberly Clark, Aekyung Industrial, LG H&H, Reckitt, and Yuhan Corp.



Assumption and Data Caveat
  • For estimating 1Q20, we assume Mar sales will be as weak as Feb sales due to ongoing virus spread in S. Korea.
  • DFS data captures foreigners’ spending on Korean brands at major Korean DFS operators. It does not capture spending on non-Korean brands and Korean locals’ spending.
  • Supermarket data captures around 20% of offline supermarket sales in S. Korea; it does not include hypermarket, conveniences stores, and online sales.
